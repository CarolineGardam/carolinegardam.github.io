Caroline Gardam

About

Caroline is a writer and editor, content producer, environmental campaigner, and communications strategist. Her work has been published nationally and internationally, including in Meanjin, Overland, and Hinterland, and her photography has been published online and is featured on the cover of The Australian Art Field: Practices, Policies, Institutions. In 2020, Caroline co-edited the anthology of Queensland writing, Our Inside Voices, a collection of over 50 essays, memoirs, poems, and stories written as an early creative response to the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020. She was a Documentation Collaborator for artist Richard Bell’s work EMBASSY2019 at Venice Biennale, and works with media organisation POPSART to produce digital content about the arts.

Caroline holds Bachelors degrees in Commerce and Arts (Psychology) and a Graduate Diploma in Arts (Writing, Editing, and Publishing) from University of Queensland. She enrolled in the Masters of Philosophy program at Queensland University of Technology in 2022 to further her understanding of digital communication, particularly regarding critical analysis of misinformation practices within digital communities.
